About Me

Aspiring Full-Stack Developer

For as long as I could remember, I saw myself working in sports. Having grown up both an avid fan and palyer of multiple sports, sports presented an outlet of creativity, competitiveness, and teamwork that allowed me to make friends while also pushing myself to my fullest potential.

Since graduating from Cornell University in 2015, I have been fortunate enough to work for three different professional sports organizations in Southern California and learned so much about the sports industry and met so many wonderful people - many of which I can call friends. With the advent of the COVID-19 pandemic, professional sports itself has continued to go on, but fans attending live events remains a distant frontier to re-visit.

In addition to sports, web development has also been a fascinating sector of techonology to me. The way that the world relies so heavily on not only the existence - but the progress of web technologies across all industries reminded me very much of how the world relies on sports. In the summer of 2020, I decided to make that leap by enrolling in LearningFuze, a web-development bootcamp based in Irvine, CA, in which I spent 14+ weeks and more than 800 hours of intense coding. I still continue to code today, as I have several projects lined up that will make an appearance here shortly. Said projects, of course, will be sports related, as I hope to bridge my two passions to showcase the unique combination of skills and knowledge.

Web development is still such a new and exciting frontier for me, one that I aspire to practice as I would in the batting cages or on the tennis courts. I truly enjoy finding new technologies and techniques to learn and develop, and look forward to the prospect of working in a team and being a meaningful contributor in the web development world for years to come.
